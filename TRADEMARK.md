# Trademark Policy — Savia and Savia Enterprise

## Scope

This document governs the use of the names **Savia** and **Savia
Enterprise**, their logos, and any derivative wordmarks. It applies to
everyone who copies, forks, repackages, or refers to this project.

The source code is MIT (see `LICENSE` and `LICENSE-ENTERPRISE.md`).
Code freedom and name freedom are separate concerns. You are free to
use, modify, and redistribute the code. You are not free to redistribute
it under the Savia name without permission.

## Names reserved for the upstream project

The following names identify the upstream project and its maintainers:

- **Savia**
- **Savia Enterprise**
- **Savia Core**
- **Savia Shield**
- **Savia Flow**
- **Savia Hub**
- **pm-workspace** (historical name of the upstream repository)

Using these names for a distinct product, service, or fork creates
confusion for users and is not permitted.

## What IS allowed without permission

- **Attribution.** You may say "powered by Savia", "based on Savia",
  or "forked from Savia" in documentation, release notes, academic
  papers, blog posts, and marketing materials, as long as the reference
  is factual and points at the upstream project.
- **Educational use.** You may use the name in tutorials, courses,
  books, conference talks, and videos that teach how Savia works.
- **Bug reports and contributions.** Issues, pull requests, and
  comments on the upstream repository are unrestricted.
- **Screenshots and demos.** You may include Savia screenshots in
  reviews, comparisons, and commentary.
- **Linking.** You may link to the Savia repository, website, or
  releases from anywhere.

## What requires a name change

If you fork the code and ship it as a distinct product, you must
**rename** the fork. You cannot publish a fork called "Savia",
"Savia Plus", "Savia Pro", "Better Savia", "Savia Next", or any name
that a reasonable user would confuse with the upstream project.

Rename examples that are fine:
- `mi-orgs-pm-stack` (no reference to Savia)
- `redwood-pm` (distinct identity)
- `orchestra` (distinct identity)

Rename examples that are not fine:
- `Savia Community Edition`
- `Savia Gold`
- `SaviaX`
- `Savia: Advanced`

The test is simple: if a user could mistake your fork for an official
release of the upstream Savia project, the name is too close.

## What is explicitly forbidden

- **Repackaging Savia as a closed product under a different name.**
  This violates the spirit of the MIT license even if it is technically
  legal. If you rename the fork to hide its origin and then sell it as
  proprietary software, you are acting in bad faith, and the upstream
  project will publicly document your fork as a repackage.
- **Claiming endorsement.** You may not claim that your product,
  service, or fork is endorsed, certified, approved, or recommended
  by the Savia project or its maintainers unless that endorsement
  exists in writing.
- **Using the Savia name for commercial services without a written
  agreement.** You cannot sell "Savia support", "Savia training",
  "Savia consulting", or "Savia certification" as your own offering
  without explicit written permission from the upstream maintainers.
  See `docs/support-offering.md` for the upstream service catalogue.
- **Domain squatting.** You may not register domains that contain
  "savia" together with software-related suffixes (`-pm`, `-ai`,
  `-enterprise`, etc.) with the intent of redirecting users away from
  the upstream project.

## Logo and wordmark

The Savia logo and wordmark are copyrighted by the upstream maintainers
and released for attribution use only. You may display them to link to
the upstream project. You may not modify them, integrate them into
derivative logos, or use them as the primary branding of a product that
is not the upstream project.

## Academic and research use

Academic papers, theses, and research artifacts may reference Savia
without restriction, including the name, the architecture, the
foundational principles, and screenshots. Standard academic citation
is appreciated but not required by this policy.

## Enforcement

The upstream maintainers will contact the operators of any project
that violates this policy before taking further action. The goal is
correction, not litigation. In most cases a rename is enough. In cases
of bad-faith repackaging, the maintainers will publicly document the
fork as a repackage so users can make informed choices.

This policy may be updated to add examples or clarify edge cases. It
will not be updated to restrict the freedoms granted here without a
public announcement and a reasonable transition window.

## Contact

To request permission for a use that falls outside this policy, open
an issue on the upstream repository with the label `trademark`.
